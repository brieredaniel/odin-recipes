Lasagna

Description

A delicious, easy to make version of the classic Italian comfort food.
Layers of hand folded pasta, creamy bechamel sauce, gooey mozzarella
and zippy bolognese will warm your heart as fast as they melt in your mouth.

Ingredients

  • Pasta
    • Flour
    • Egg Yolk
    • Water
    • Olive Oil
    • Salt
  • Bechamel
    • Butter
    • Flour
    • Salt
    • Milk
    • Nutmeg
  • Bolognese
    • Anchovies
    • Olive Oil
    • Onion
    • Garlic
    • Carrot
    • Celery
    • Ground Pork and/or Beef
    • Crushed Tomatoes
    • Balsamic Vinegar
    • Salt
    • Fresh Basil
  • Freshly Grated Mozzarella
  • Ricotta Cheese (Fresh is best)
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan
  • Chopped Fresh Herbs for garnish

Steps

  1. Begin by preheating the oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Combine pasta ingredients and knead to make dough. Texture should be smooth, pliable, and firm. Roll into a ball and wrap in cello until ready to roll into noodles.
  3. Make Bolognese. Melt anchovies in a splash of olive oil on medium-high heat, then add diced onions and garlic. Add ground meat, carrots and celery. Stir until meat
    is browned, then cover for 3 minutes, until onions and celery begin to become soft. Season with salt and pepper. Add crushed tomatoes, a splash of balsamic vinegar, stir,
    and bring to a boil. Cover and reduce heat, let simmer for 30 min.
  4. Make Bechamel. Melt butter over medium heat, add flour and milk alternately, in small amounts. Once a nice sauce has formed, season with salt and nutmeg.
  5. Put a large pot of water on to boil--heavily seasoned with salt--while you make the noodles. Uncover the ball of past dough, and roll it out into thin sheets. This can
    be done by hand, or with a pasta maker, or a combination of both. Cut sheets into 8 inch lengths, then drop the pasta into the boiling water. When the noodles float after
    90 seconds or so, remove them and pat them dry with paper towel.
  6. Assemble the lasgna in pyrex baking dish. Start with a thin layer of sauce, then layer noodles to cover the bottom of the dish. Alternate bolognese, ricotta, noodles,
    bechamel, parmesan, noodles, etc. Top liberally with mozzarella and parmesan, and sprinkle fresh herbs for garnish.
  7. Cover with aluminium foil and bake in the oven for 45 minutes. In the last 10 minutes, remove foil to brown mozza and crisp the edges.
  8. Allow lasagna to cool for 15 minutes or so before serving. And enjoy!
